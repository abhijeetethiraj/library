friction and its reduction

Friction is a force that occurs when two surfaces slide against each other, caused by the irregularities on the surfaces becoming entangled. Friction can be reduced by making surfaces smoother, using lubricants, or streamlining objects: Smoothing surfaces: Polishing surfaces can make them smoother, reducing friction. Using lubricants: Lubricants like oil, grease, or graphite can fill in gaps between surfaces, reducing friction. Streamlining objects: The shape of an object can affect air resistance, which can slow it down.
